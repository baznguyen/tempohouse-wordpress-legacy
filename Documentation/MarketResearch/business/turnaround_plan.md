## Executive Situation Assessment

TEMPO House is a genuinely differentiated hospitality concept — specialty café, craft cocktail bar, rotating art gallery, and private event hire under one French-colonial roof on Pasteur Street, District 3 — operating with near-zero commercial infrastructure in a market where it should be winning. The business is in debt because it has built the product without building the machine that sells it: no Google Business Profile, no booking system, no event CMS, no Vietnamese content, no B2B sales motion, and zero editorial presence in any publication its target audience reads. The highest-margin revenue stream (private event hire) is effectively closed to corporate buyers because the intake mechanism is a generic contact form with no pricing, no qualifying fields, and no follow-up process. This is not a concept failure — it is a distribution failure, and almost every gap causing revenue underperformance can be addressed in under 60 days at near-zero cost.

## The 5 Highest-Priority Actions (Ranked)

### Priority 1 — Create and Verify the Google Business Profile
**Why it is first:** Every other marketing action requires this to have been done. TEMPO does not appear in Google Maps for any District 3 search query. This is the primary discovery channel for both walk-in traffic and event enquiries in HCMC. Not having it is equivalent to operating without a sign on the door.

**Specific actions:**
- Claim 218c Pasteur Street, District 3 in Google Business Profile
- Set primary category: Event Venue. Secondary categories: Coffee Shop, Cocktail Bar, Art Gallery, Party Venue
- Upload minimum 20 photos across all four venue modes (café morning light, gallery afternoon, bar evening, event configuration)
- Write a 700-character bilingual description (English and Vietnamese) anchoring the Reunification Palace proximity, the Aussie-Vietnamese founders, and the rotating gallery
- Set dual opening hours: café 07:00–17:00, bar 18:00–01:00
- Seed the Q&A section with the 10 most likely questions (parking, event hire minimum spend, capacity, gallery access)
- Schedule weekly GMB posts about upcoming gallery shows and events once the events CMS is live

**Time to impact:** Map pack appearances within 2–4 weeks of verification.  
**Cost:** Zero.

---

### Priority 2 — Build and Deploy the Private Events Commercial Infrastructure
**Why it is second:** Private event hire is the fastest path to meaningful cash in this debt situation. A single full-venue evening event at market rates generates more gross profit than multiple days of café and bar combined. The product exists. The building is there. What does not exist is the machinery to sell it.

**Specific actions:**
- Draft a one-page venue hire rate card (internal, not public-facing initially): three configurations (café floor 60 seated / gallery Level 1 80 standing / full venue 150+ standing), a pricing band per configuration, minimum spend requirement, 30% non-refundable deposit on booking, and an inclusions list
- Create a 4–6 page venue capabilities PDF (external): gallery photography in event mode, capacity specs, floor configuration options, F&B package outline, AV notes, catering partnership options, and direct enquiry contact
- Replace the current /events/enquiry contact form with a structured qualifying form: name, company, event type (dropdown), preferred date, approximate guest count, estimated budget range (dropdown), catering needs, notes. Submissions trigger immediate email notification to the team and an auto-responder to the enquirer within 60 seconds
- Build dedicated event sub-pages: /events/corporate-events, /events/product-launch, /events/brand-activation, /events/year-end-party, /events/birthday-celebration, /events/intimate-weddings — each with keyword-specific copy targeting the Tier 1 event hire keyword atlas, correct meta, H1, and a direct path to the enquiry form
- Personally email the venue capabilities PDF to 20 named marketing managers, PR agency heads, and brand activation leads at HCMC companies — this is direct outreach from a founder, not a mail merge. Reference the recipient's brand specifically. Target: wine importers, fashion houses, advertising agencies, design studios, technology companies, PR firms

**Time to first booking:** 14–30 days from outreach.  
**Cost:** Zero (founder time only).

### Priority 3 — Install Analytics and Technical SEO Foundation
**Why it is third:** The business is making marketing decisions without any data. Every day without tracking is data permanently lost. Technical SEO gaps (schema markup, sitemap, Open Graph tags) are costing organic ranking for zero additional effort once done.

**Specific actions:**
- Install Google Analytics 4 with goal tracking for: enquiry form submissions, reservation attempts, newsletter sign-ups, PDF downloads, and CTA clicks
- Connect Google Search Console and submit sitemap.xml
- Add JSON-LD structured data across all pages: LocalBusiness + FoodEstablishment + ArtGallery on homepage, Restaurant on /cafe and /bar, EventVenue on /events, Event schema on every individual /whats-on listing, BreadcrumbList on all sub-pages
- Add Open Graph and Twitter Card meta tags to every page (og:title, og:description, og:image at 1200×630px, og:type)
- Audit image filenames and alt text for keyword alignment across all existing site photography
- Run PageSpeed Insights on the current site and self-host Monument Grotesk and Editorial New fonts rather than serving from a remote CDN — this is the single largest mobile performance quick-win

**Time to impact:** Schema markup and Core Web Vitals improvements register in Google Search Console within 2–3 weeks.  
**Cost:** Zero.

**Why it is fourth:** 80–85% of HCMC hospitality searches happen in Vietnamese. TEMPO does not exist on the platforms where local Vietnamese consumers discover cafés, bars, and event venues. This is a structural exclusion from the primary local market that is corrected in under 48 hours at zero cost.

**Specific actions:**

- Zalopay.vn merchant listing

- Contact acchcm.vn directly to request inclusion in their "top cafés on Pasteur Street District 3" article — this is the only Vietnamese-language editorial piece targeting TEMPO's exact geographic position

**Time to impact:** First Vietnamese organic discovery traffic within 30 days.  
**Cost:** Zero.

### Priority 5 — Build the Journal/Content CMS and Publish the First 6 Articles
**Why it is fifth:** The entire SEO strategy has nowhere to live. The Tier 1 keyword clusters identified in the research (specialty coffee D3, Pasteur Street café, café near Reunification Palace, art gallery café Saigon, boutique event venue D3) are all near-zero competition — any competent article targeting these terms can rank within 60–90 days. But there is no /journal section on the website, so none of this is possible until the CMS is built.

**Specific actions:**
- Build a WordPress blog CMS at /journal with clean URL structure (/journal/[slug]), no dates in URL, and editorial categories aligned to the five brand content pillars
- Publish the six founding articles at launch:
  1. "Specialty Cafés on Pasteur Street, District 3, Saigon" — claims the geographic territory in English; near-zero competition
  2. "The Café 500 Metres from the Reunification Palace" — captures tourist search intent; entirely uncontested keyword
  3. "Rotating Art Gallery Cafés in Ho Chi Minh City" — claims the Saigon Manzi position; no competitor has published on this
  4. "A Considered Evening on Pasteur Street, District 3" — the bar and evening angle; targets hidden bar and cocktail bar D3 terms
  5. "Creative Event Venue Hire in District 3 HCMC — What to Know" — event hire SEO content with commercial intent
  6. "The Founder's Note: Why This Address, Why District 3, Why Now" — brand editorial; serves press pitch and About page purposes simultaneously
- Each article carries Event or LocalBusiness schema where relevant, internal links to conversion pages, and is submitted to Search Console on publication

**Time to first ranking:** 60–90 days per article for Tier 1 keywords.  
**Cost:** Founder writing time only. No paid content required.

## Additional Structural Recommendations (Non-Priority-Order)

**On the debt position:** Model the private events revenue requirement explicitly — if debt service requires X VND per month and a full-venue event delivers Y VND in gross margin, the business needs Z events per month. This number should be the operational north star, not a vague ambition. Assign one person to own the events pipeline; this stream will not self-activate.

**On cost reduction:** Do not cut gallery programming, specialty coffee sourcing, or the cocktail programme — these are the margin-justifying assets. Pause any paid social advertising spend that is not directly tied to a booking path. Zero-base the marketing budget: nothing gets spent unless there is a measurable conversion path attached to it.

**On the gallery:** Formalise the commission structure with exhibiting artists immediately (industry standard: 40–50% on works sold). This converts a programming overhead into a revenue line with zero additional cost.

**On Vietnamese content:** The bilingual Aussie-Vietnamese founding identity is being systematically underexploited. The founders are not foreign operators — they are returning Vietnamese Australians building for the city they came home to. This framing is the most credible press hook in the venue's arsenal and should lead every Vietnamese-language communication, every press pitch, and the About page headline.

**On font licensing:** Monument Grotesk and Editorial New (Pangram Pangram) require commercial web licensing for the tempohouse.com.vn deployment. Resolve this within 30 days — a cease-and-desist at the moment TEMPO gains editorial coverage is a worst-case scenario that is entirely preventable.

**On De La Sol:** 244 Pasteur, 26 doors away, Sun Life Insurance-backed, currently ranking for Vietnamese art-café search terms on the same street. Monitor their content output monthly. Every piece of TEMPO's gallery programming that is documented and published is a direct rebuttal of their corporate art-washing — the creative community will draw the comparison unprompted. Never name De La Sol in any content; instead describe TEMPO as "artist-programmed, not brand-decorated."
