## What This File Is For

This is a living audit of how competing and benchmark venues write copy — globally and locally. The goal is not to imitate, but to understand what moves, why it works, and what TEMPO must do differently to occupy unclaimed linguistic territory.

---

## Global Benchmark: How the Best Venues Write

### Soho House (Global)
**Their register:** Members-first, insider-without-arrogance, presumes familiarity.
> *"A place for people in the creative industries to meet, work and relax."*
> *"No two Houses are the same."*

**Lessons for TEMPO:**
- Directness works. No adjectives decorating what something is — just say what it is.
- "No two X are the same" construction positions the space as living, not fixed. ("No two evenings share the same walls" is TEMPO's version.)
- They never explain the concept. They invite you to already understand it.

**What NOT to copy:** The membership/exclusivity framing. TEMPO is open. The welcome is the point.

---

### The Ned, London
**Their register:** Glamorous precision. Uses specific details (the year, the architect) as proof of provenance.
> *"The Ned opened in 2017 in the former Midland Bank building designed by Sir Edwin Lutyens."*
> *"Nine restaurants and bars. One hotel."*

**Lessons for TEMPO:**
- Specific physical details (French-colonial shophouse, 218c Pasteur, exposed brick, original timber) carry more credibility than claims.
- Their "nine restaurants and bars, one hotel" — that compression. TEMPO equivalent: "One room. Four lives."
- They do not say "incredible" or "stunning." The space proves itself.

---

### Ace Hotel, New York / Portland
**Their register:** Anti-hospitality. Culturally grounded, local references, lowercase aesthetic, refuses the luxury template.
> *"the ace hotel has been a meeting place, a crash pad, a lobby for the unaffiliated."*

**Lessons for TEMPO:**
- Permission to lower the case. "tempo house" not "TEMPO House" in editorial contexts.
- "For the unaffiliated" — there's a TEMPO equivalent here. The space claims its crowd without naming them.
- They write about TIME — what happens when, not just what the space is.

---

### Tate Modern / Tate Britain (London)
**Their register:** Editorial authority. Speaks of art with precision, not reverence.
> *"Artists have always responded to their times."*
> *"Turbine Hall: 3,300 square metres. No permanent collection."*

**Lessons for TEMPO:**
- Writing about art without mystifying it. "The gallery changes. The work rotates. Nothing hangs forever." — that's TEMPO's version.
- Specific dimensions, specific facts. Not "a stunning space" but "3,300 square metres."
- The gallery level copy should never say "curated" or "immersive." Specific artist name + what the work is + when it changes.

---

### APW Bangsar, Kuala Lumpur
**Their register:** Creative district venue with a fashion-adjacent voice. Understated.
> *"APW was a printing company. Now it's a neighbourhood."*

**Lessons for TEMPO:**
- Historical anchor + present use. "218c was a French-colonial shophouse. It still is." reads as TEMPO equivalent.
- "A neighbourhood" — the venue as a community node, not just a building.

---

### Smoke & Mirrors, Singapore
**Their register:** Nighttime, exclusive, precise. Every word earns its place.
> *"Saigon's best kept secret" — they would never say this. They just are the secret.*

**Lessons for TEMPO:**
- The bar doesn't name itself the best. It writes like it is, and lets others name it.
- Their cocktail descriptions are precise (ingredient-first, technique-second, not "crafted with love").

---

### Manzi Art Space & Café, Hanoi (closest format benchmark)
**Their register:** Warm but spare. Bilingual (Vietnamese + English). Art-first but accessible.
> *"A space for art, music, and conversation."*

**Lessons for TEMPO:**
- The simplicity of the tagline is the point. It doesn't over-explain.
- Bilingual approach from the start — Vietnamese and English are equal. TEMPO currently has zero Vietnamese copy.
- "A space for" — TEMPO can own a more specific version: "A room for the morning you need to think and the evening that earns its end."

## Saigon Local Competitor Profiles

### De La Sol (244 Pasteur, District 3)
**Their copy register:** Corporate-friendly, event-first, pseudo-creative.

Key phrases they use:
- "Creative experience space"
- "Where art meets lifestyle"  
- "Curated exhibitions"
- Monthly event partnerships (Sun Life Insurance, corporate brands)

**TEMPO attack angle in copy:**
- Never use "curated" (they do). Never say "lifestyle" (they do).
- Artist names, not brand names. Works, not partnerships.
- "We don't decorate. We program." — the unspoken differentiation.

**Warning:** De La Sol has strong Vietnamese-language content and Lemon8 viral posts. TEMPO has nothing in Vietnamese. This is the most urgent content gap vs. nearest competitor.

---

### SOMA (District 4, HCMC)
**Their register:** Emerging artist positioning. Accessible, community-forward, slightly earnest.
> "Supporting emerging Vietnamese artists."

**TEMPO differentiation:** TEMPO is not a support structure — it's a permanent condition. Art isn't the cause, it's the atmosphere. Different register, different audience.

---

### XLIII Coffee (District 3)
**Their register:** Specialty coffee craft. Third-wave, process-focused.
- Strong Vietnamese Instagram presence
- No events, no bar
- Overlaps only on morning specialty coffee use case

**Competitive note:** On specialty coffee alone, XLIII is strong. TEMPO wins by being more than coffee — the arc of the day extends past the cup.

---

### The Factory Contemporary Arts Centre (District 2)
**Their register:** Institutional. Grant-supported. Educational programming language.

**TEMPO differentiation:** The Factory is a gallery you visit. TEMPO is a gallery you inhabit while having your morning coffee or evening drink. Different intent, different moment.

## Copy Principles for TEMPO (Derived from Audit)

### 1. Write like a magazine editor, not a hospitality copywriter
Magazine editors state. Hospitality copywriters enthuse.
- ❌ "An incredible multi-concept space where art meets cocktails"
- ✅ "Three floors. One address. It changes when the light does."

### 2. Use specifics as proof
Physical facts replace adjectives.
- ❌ "A beautifully restored heritage building"
- ✅ "A 1930s shophouse on Pasteur Street. The ceiling is 4.2 metres. The walls remember several previous lives."

### 3. Time is the concept, not a detail
The "different tempos" idea is structural, not descriptive. It should appear in the architecture of the page, not in a tagline.
- ❌ "Visit us from 7am to 1am"
- ✅ "The morning crowd is gone by noon. The evening crowd doesn't know about the morning. That's how we like it."

### 4. Never explain the concept
A concept that needs explaining hasn't landed yet. Write to the reader who already gets it, and let the uninitiated want in.

### 5. Short sentences. Declarative. No qualifiers.
> "The art changes. The company changes. The pace doesn't."
> "Not a scene. A standard."

### 6. Address the reader directly — or not at all
Second person (you/your) or third person (they/the crowd) — both work. First person plural (we) is the brand voice. Never passive.

## Forbidden Copy Patterns (What Every Competitor Does)

These phrases appear in 80%+ of Saigon venue copy. Using any of these marks TEMPO as indistinguishable:

| Pattern | Why it fails |
|---|---|
| "…where art meets…" | De La Sol's exact territory |
| "curated [experience/space/menu]" | Overused across all competitors |
| "an immersive experience" | Explains itself out of interest |
| "Saigon's hidden gem" | Self-defeating (if hidden, you can't say so) |
| "for creative minds" | Exclusionary in a way that's also a cliché |
| "where community comes together" | Generic, zero specificity |
| "a unique space" | Every competitor claims this |
| "craft cocktails in a relaxed atmosphere" | Every D1 bar, every F&B listing |
| "vibrant arts scene" | Tourism board language |
| "passionate team" | Meaningless in any context |
