## Tempo Rhythm Strategy

**Headline Concept:** One Address, Three Revenue Channels, Seven Revenue Opportunities — the 'Different Tempos Throughout the Day' concept is not a brand story, it is a commercial architecture. The morning is a hospitality product. The evening is a premium hospitality product. The gallery is a sponsorship vehicle. The full venue is an event hire product. Each tempo of the day is a distinct revenue line with distinct buyers, distinct conversion paths, and distinct pricing logic. Marketing this concept means selling seven different products under one brand — and the power of the unified brand is that each product makes the others more valuable.

**Business Implications:** The 'different tempos throughout the day' positioning, when commercialised correctly, transforms TEMPO from a hospitality business (selling drinks and coffee) into a commercial platform (selling time, space, and cultural credibility to businesses). A hospitality business has variable revenue that depends on foot traffic. A commercial platform has forecastable revenue from B2B contracts, sponsorship deals, and long-term venue hire agreements. The path from debt to stability runs through the B2B channel — not through increasing café covers.

### Implementation Ideas
- Create a 'One Building, Four Modes' sales deck — a 6-slide visual that shows the same space at 08:00, 14:00, 19:00, and during a private event. Send this to every corporate event planner contact as the opening salvo. No venue in HCMC has a sales deck that shows physical transformation this clearly — this is the B2B conversion tool that replaces a site visit for planners in early shortlisting stages.
- Build a 'Day Rate / Evening Rate / Hire Rate' pricing page that explicitly shows how the space transitions — daytime hire (café floor, 60 seats, morning briefing format), gallery hire (Level 1, 80 standing, product launch format), full venue hire (150+ standing, evening event format). Each rate tier should have a 'this is right for you if...' qualifier so planners self-select. Transparent pricing is the single biggest conversion lever in the event venue market.
- Launch a 'Brand Partner' programme — approach 3-5 brands whose values align with TEMPO's creative positioning (coffee roasters, spirits brands, design-adjacent consumer brands) to sponsor exhibition cycles. Sponsorship value: naming rights on the opening night invitation, logo on the gallery wall for the exhibition duration, co-branded content, and two complimentary event evenings in the space. Price: 20-40M VND per exhibition cycle. This turns the gallery overhead into a recurring revenue line without changing anything about the programming.
- Develop a 'Morning to Midnight' event format specifically marketed to advertising agencies and creative companies — full-day hire where the agency uses the café in the morning for a team workshop, the gallery at midday for a client presentation, and the bar in the evening for a client dinner. This is a single-booking, full-day revenue event that no competitor can structurally replicate because no competitor has all three spaces under one roof.
- Create a 'Location on Demand' offering for production companies, content studios, and photographers — hourly rate for the space as a shoot location. The French-colonial architecture, gallery walls, bar setup, and café counter are each a distinct 'set' with genuine production value. Market this through LinkedIn to HCMC-based production studios, advertising agencies (who need location options for campaigns), and KOL agencies (who book locations for content creators). This generates revenue in the gap hours (early morning, mid-afternoon) when the space is not at full hospitality capacity.
- Build a 'Rhythm of the Quarter' events calendar that is pitched to corporate clients 90 days in advance — product launches in Q3, YEP in Q4, client entertainment in Q1, team offsite in Q2. Each quarter has a signature event format, a package price, and a booking window. Pitching this calendar to a corporate event planner in one meeting closes the whole year's business rather than one booking at a time.
