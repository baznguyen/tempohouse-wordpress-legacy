## Tempo Rhythm Strategy

**Headline Concept:** The Room That Earns Different Things at Different Hours — marketing TEMPO House not as a multi-venue hybrid but as a single, coherent address whose character deepens across the day rather than switching between separate products. The concept positions TEMPO as the only place in Saigon where the same square metres produce morning focus, afternoon contemplation, evening conversation, and the art that holds it all together — because the building's identity is its rhythm, not its menu.

**Business Implications:** The 'different tempos throughout the day' positioning is not just a marketing concept — it is the business model's primary justification for its operational complexity. A café that becomes a bar at night is a liability; a building that earns differently at different hours is an asset. The marketing strategy must reflect this distinction clearly, because it is what justifies the multi-staff model, the gallery programming investment, and the premium pricing across all four revenue streams. Critically, the tempo concept is also the primary B2B sales tool: a corporate event client who understands that TEMPO's building actively configures itself around the occasion — and that their guests will experience the building as a deliberate host rather than a hired room — will pay a premium and refer others. The rhythm concept converts event hire from a commodity transaction into a brand experience sale, which is what allows TEMPO to compete with hotel function rooms on positioning rather than price.

### Implementation Ideas
- TikTok series 'One Address' — a recurring video format showing the same physical elements of TEMPO (the bar counter, the staircase, the gallery wall) at different hours of the same day; no voiceover, strong ambient sound design, 30 to 45 seconds per episode; the structural repeating format rewards algorithm engagement and teaches the audience the concept without explaining it
- The time-stamped Instagram content system — every Instagram post carries a timestamp in the caption or visual that anchors it to a specific hour of the day: '07:42 — the first pour of the morning', '14:15 — the gallery is quiet', '21:30 — the room has changed'; this system makes the tempo-through-the-day concept visible in the feed without requiring a caption to explain it

- A 'Tempo Map' physical card left on café tables — a small, well-designed card that maps the building's moods across the day with minimal copy; something a visitor picks up, reads in ten seconds, and either keeps or photographs; functions as a retention tool, a social content prompt, and a physical extension of the brand
