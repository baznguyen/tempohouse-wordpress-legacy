## RAG Audit — Full Dimension Scores

### Visual Health — 🟢 GREEN

The strongest dimension at launch.

- **Cream + Terracotta + Sand** reads as warm, analogue, anti-digital. Exactly right for a Melbourne-influenced specialty café with Vietnamese warmth.
- **Charcoal + Amber** gives night mode natural authority without the lazy black-and-neon speakeasy visual cliché.
- **Sage** is the quiet star — adds organic credibility, stops the palette becoming purely warm/nostalgic. It's the gallery note in the colour system.
- **Type stack** (Monument Grotesk / Space Grotesk / Editorial New) is sophisticated and coherent. Can carry the morning coffee menu and late-night cocktail list without contradiction.

**Risks:**
- No logo reviewed — the entire visual system is unanchored without this.
- No colour activation map — which tones lead in which mode?
- Monument Grotesk is appearing in premium hospitality globally; window of ownable distinctiveness narrowing. Use it boldly or it becomes wallpaper.

**Recommendations:**
- Prioritise logo review before any print or environmental production.
- Create colour activation guide: Day leads with Cream/Sand/Sage/Terracotta. Night leads with Charcoal/Amber, Terracotta as accent, Cream as negative space.
- Introduce a graphic language beyond type and colour — a motif or compositional rule uniquely TEMPO's. Consider *rhythm* expressed visually: intervals, tempo marks, breath between elements.

### Voice & Tone — 🟢 GREEN

"Economical, editorial, anti-trend" is not just a tone description — it's a brand value. In HCMC's hospitality market, TEMPO's stated voice occupies real white space.

**Strong:**
> *"Coffee in the morning. Connection at night."* — clean, confident, earns its simplicity.
> *"Stay for one. Stay for many."* — works harder than it looks. Covers dwell time, social occasion, and the invitation to return.

**Weak:**
> *"Find your rhythm."* — **This is a brand-killer.** It appears across wellness, fitness, and lifestyle brands globally. Directly contradicts the anti-trend mandate. It is placeholder thinking dressed as a tagline and must be replaced before launch.

**Risks:**
- No Vietnamese-language voice guidelines. This is a significant gap. A brand that operates only in English in Ho Chi Minh City makes a cultural statement — intentional or not.
- Anti-trend copy can tip into self-conscious coolness, which *is* a trend. The voice needs to be **genuinely unhurried**, not performatively unhurried.

**Forbidden words list (TEMPO never uses):**
curated, artisanal, vibe, amazing, awesome, immersive, journey, atmosphere, passionate about, unforgettable, experience (as a noun)

### Brand Consistency — 🟡 AMBER

Strong concept, but multi-format risk needs structural resolution.

The day/night theming is a smart structural decision. The name TEMPO does meaningful work: rhythm, pace, timing, musical cadence. It holds across both modes.

**Risk:** Three distinct offerings (café / speakeasy / gallery) risk reading as brand fragmentation rather than brand richness if the connective tissue isn't explicit. Day/night theming must be executed as **one brand in two moods**, not two brands sharing a postcode.

**Recommendations:**
- Define a **Brand Spine** — one sentence that holds across day and night, café and gallery.
- Formalise the day/night system as **TEMPO Day** and **TEMPO Night** with explicit rules: which palette values activate when, type hierarchy shifts, tone register changes.
- Treat the Level 2 gallery as **TEMPO Gallery** — a named sub-brand with its own event identity that still draws from the master palette and type system.

## Brand Personality — 5 Pillars

### 1. Unhurried
- Sounds like: A barista who explains the process once, clearly, without performing it.
- Feels like: Knowing you don't need to rush your second coffee.
- Never: Slow service dressed up as ambience.

### 2. Exacting
- Sounds like: A menu with fewer items than expected, each one deliberate.
- Feels like: The confidence of a room that's been edited, not decorated.
- Never: Perfectionism that makes guests feel judged.

### 3. Dual-natured
- Sounds like: The same space that held your 10am focus holds your 10pm conversation — no apology, no explanation.
- Feels like: A place that knows you at different hours.
- Never: A split identity. Day and night are one continuous character arc.

### 4. Rooted
- Sounds like: Melbourne-trained, Saigon-made. The provenance is in the work, not the branding.
- Feels like: A venue that belongs here — not transplanted, not nostalgic.
- Never: Expat nostalgia or Vietnamese-as-backdrop aesthetics.

### 5. Quietly generous
- Sounds like: The kind of host who refills your water before you notice it's low.
- Feels like: Community built through attention, not programming.
- Never: Performative hospitality. No "amazing" or "awesome."

## Brand Voice Guide

**Governing principle:** TEMPO House writes like a person who has strong opinions and shares them without insisting you agree. Editorial, not instructional. Warm, not effusive. Always specific — never vague gestures toward "atmosphere" or "vibes."

### Voice by Mode

**Day register (cafe hours):**
> *"The 08:00 crowd knows what they're here for. Pull up a stool or claim the corner. We'll keep the music low until you don't need it to be."*

**Night register (bar hours):**
> *"The lights don't change dramatically. The room just shifts. You'll notice it around the second drink."*

**Gallery/Events:**
> *"Level 2 is open for the current exhibition. There's no guided tour. Stay as long as the work asks you to."*

### Writing Principles

**Earn every adjective.** Descriptors must do actual work. If the adjective could describe any café in District 1, cut it.

> ❌ *"A warm, welcoming space where creativity flows."*
> ✅ *"Two floors. Three modes. One address that works harder than your schedule."*

**Write in civilian time, not hospitality time.**

> ❌ *"Our team is passionate about bringing you an unforgettable coffee experience."*
> ✅ *"The coffee is made by people who've spent years getting this specific. You'll taste the difference by the third sip, not the first."*

**Let the format do the talking.** Trust readers to feel the transition between day and night — don't explain it.

## Visual Identity Direction for Wireframing

### Colour Activation Map

| Mode | Leading Palette | Psychological Register |
|---|---|---|
| **Day (Cafe)** | Cream `#F7F3EE`, Sand `#E7D8C9`, Sage `#8A9277`, Terracotta `#C76E4B` accent | Focus, warmth, editorial calm. Natural light welcomed. Nothing shouts. |
| **Transition (Dusk)** | Terracotta deepening toward Amber | The hour when the room earns its second personality |
| **Night (Bar/Gallery)** | Ink `#1A1816`, Charcoal `#2C2C2C`, Amber `#DDAA62`, Cream as negative space | Intimate, precise, considered. Dark without being heavy. |

**Rule:** The palette doesn't switch — it deepens. Day and night exist on the same tonal spectrum.

### Typography Hierarchy

- **Headlines:** Monument Grotesk — authority, contemporary. Use at weight. Let it breathe.
- **Body:** Space Grotesk — utility, digital-ready. Should disappear into readability.
- **Accent/Editorial moments:** Editorial New italic — menu descriptions, pullquotes, gallery labels, special moments.
- **Functional info (times, prices, event details):** Consider a monospaced or condensed face — signals precision without clinical coldness.

### Imagery Style

**Pursue:**
- Medium-close documentary. The espresso being placed, not posed.
- Available light, always. Morning haze through windows. Amber lamp spill on a dark table.
- People in conversation, not performance — mid-thought, mid-laugh, mid-sentence.
- The space between things: an empty stool at 8am, a half-finished drink at midnight, the gallery wall before the opening crowd arrives.

**Avoid:**
- Overhead flat-lays. Golden-hour influencer tableaux. Drone shots. Artificial filters.

## Audience Personas for Wireframing

### Primary: The HCMC Multi-Hyphenate
Vietnamese creatives and professionals aged 27-40. Designers, strategists, founders, writers, architects, creative directors. Bilingual by default. Chooses venues the way they curate their feeds — slowly, loyally, with high standards. Wants: morning focus without interruption, evening conversation without venue-hopping, art to linger in front of without a curator talking at them.

### Secondary: The HCMC Expat Creative
International creatives aged 28-45. Comes from strong hospitality cultures (Melbourne, London, Tokyo, Berlin, Seoul). Has found places they like in HCMC but not the full stack under one roof. Wants: familiarity of craft, coffee that tastes like a place they trust, cocktails with a point of view, permission to stay all day without ordering on a schedule.

### Tertiary: Cultural Event Seeker
Enters through Level 2 (gallery/events). Converts to regulars through the experience of the full venue. B2B event clients also sit here.

## Website Wireframing Implications

Based on this brand analysis, the website structure should:

1. **Communicate all three layers** (cafe / bar / gallery) without making them feel like separate venues — one narrative, three modes.

2. **Day/Night mode toggle is a first-class UI feature** — not an easter egg or accessibility setting. It should be prominent, intuitive, and feel like discovering the venue at a different hour.

3. **The gallery needs event-grade presentation** — not a sidebar. Own a full section with real photography, exhibition info, and a private events enquiry path.

4. **Low-friction events/functions enquiry** — this is the highest-margin revenue stream. The path from "I want to host something" to "I've submitted an enquiry" should be 2 taps maximum.

5. **Bilingual from day one** — Vietnamese and English as integrated voices, not as a language toggle. Copy should be written in both languages authentically, not translated.

6. **The founders' story earns a real page** — the Aussie-Vietnamese dual background is the most credible differentiator. It should be told warmly, specifically, and without the word "passionate."

7. **No "coming soon" energy** — even in pre-launch, the site should feel like a venue that is ready and knows what it is. The current "coming soon" placeholder should be replaced as soon as wireframes are locked.

## Brand Story (Muse-authored)

**Origin:** Two Aussie-Vietnamese creatives grew up between Melbourne's café culture and HCMC's creative energy — inheritors of both, native to neither, fluent in each. They came back to Saigon not to import a model but to build something that only this specific intersection of experience could produce. TEMPO isn't Melbourne in Vietnam. It's what happens when people who understand hospitality deeply choose to make it somewhere that matters to them personally.

**Mission:** To hold space — literally and figuratively — for the creative community that's building this city's next chapter. A place where the quality of your coffee is taken as seriously as the quality of the work being shown upstairs.

**Aspiration:** In five years, TEMPO House is the address that HCMC creatives give to people who are just arriving in the city and ask where to go. Not because it's fashionable, but because it's reliably, consistently itself — and that has become rare enough to feel like a gift.
