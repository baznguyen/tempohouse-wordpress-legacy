## 1. About TEMPO House

TEMPO House is an experience-first hospitality venue in Ho Chi Minh City, Vietnam. It operates as:

- **A Melbourne-style specialty cafe** — mornings and afternoons, slow-paced, craft coffee, warmth, natural light
- **An intimate speakeasy cocktail bar** — evenings and nights, ambient, quality-led, community-centred
- **A Level 2 art gallery and event space** — a curated creative venue for exhibitions, private functions, brand activations, and workshops

The concept is not a cafe that moonlights as a bar. It is a coherent, day-to-night cultural space built around a single unifying idea: **people live at different rhythms, and great spaces adapt to them.**

TEMPO occupies a genuine whitespace in HCMC's hospitality market — a place that is simultaneously warm, intelligent, culturally grounded, and operationally ambitious.

## 4. Business Model — The Three-Layer Stack

TEMPO's model is three revenue-generating layers sharing one address and one brand. Each has distinct economics, audience dynamics, and operating requirements.

### Layer 1 — Morning/Afternoon Cafe (Ground Floor)

| | |
|---|---|
| **Hours** | 7am – 5pm (primary), with afternoon crossover |
| **Offering** | Specialty coffee (Melbourne craft standard), light food, all-day menu |
| **Price point** | Premium specialty tier — 15–25% above market average |
| **Revenue driver** | Dwell time × ticket average × repeat visit rate |
| **Differentiator** | Warmth, craft, cultural depth — not just third-wave minimalism |
| **Target customer** | Remote workers, creatives, multi-hyphenates, culture-aware professionals |

**Operating logic:** The cafe succeeds on regulars, not footfall. One customer who visits 4x/week is worth more than four different customers who come once. Everything — staffing warmth, music, lighting, menu — must be engineered to earn the repeat.

### Layer 2 — Evening Speakeasy Bar (Ground Floor)

| | |
|---|---|
| **Hours** | 6pm – midnight+ |
| **Offering** | Craft cocktails, curated wine, small plates, spirits |
| **Price point** | Premium craft bar tier — 200,000–450,000 VND per drink |
| **Revenue driver** | Occupancy rate × spend per head × bar margin (~65–75%) |
| **Differentiator** | Intimate, warm, intelligent — the opposite of loud HCMC nightlife |
| **Target customer** | Thoughtful socialisers, date nights, post-work drinks, creative community |

**Operating logic:** An intimate bar running at 75% capacity with high spend per head will significantly outperform a large venue at 40% capacity. The speakeasy format — discovered, curated, never forced — creates the word-of-mouth engine that makes it sustainable.

### Layer 3 — Level 2 Art Gallery & Event Space

| | |
|---|---|
| **Hours** | Flexible — events, exhibitions, functions |
| **Offering** | Venue hire, curated exhibitions, F&B packages, workshops, brand activations |
| **Price point** | B2B pricing — minimum spends + hire fees |
| **Revenue driver** | Booking rate × package value + gallery commissions (30–50% on art sales) |
| **Differentiator** | Curated, not just available — a gallery with a point of view |
| **Target customer** | Brands seeking premium activation space, creative community events, private functions, art collectors |

**Operating logic:** This layer has the highest margin per event and the most defensible competitive position. No other venue in HCMC combines genuine gallery curation with quality F&B in a space this intimate. Requires dedicated B2B sales effort — it will not self-book.

### Day-to-Night Flow

```
MORNING TEMPO        AFTERNOON TEMPO       NIGHT TEMPO
Soft. Slow.          Social. Creative.     Warm. Intimate.
─────────────────    ──────────────────    ────────────────
Coffee rituals       Meetings, collabs     Cocktails, wine
Quiet focus          Workshops, brand      Small gatherings
Warm light           activations           Deeper ambience
7am → 11am           11am → 6pm            6pm → midnight+
```

The transition windows (late morning, late afternoon) are programming opportunities — events, workshops, and tastings that bridge the modes create content and deepen community engagement.

## 6. Market Context — HCMC F&B Landscape

### The Market at a Glance

| Metric | Data |
|---|---|
| HCMC population | ~10 million (metro area ~14 million) |
| Estimated F&B establishments | 20,000+ |
| New venue survival rate (18 months) | ~60–70% failure |
| Specialty coffee market maturity | High — third-wave arrived ~2015, now mainstream |
| Craft cocktail bar market | Growing — significant since 2019 |
| International visitor arrivals 2025 | ~6 million to HCMC |
| Expat population | ~80,000–100,000 |
| Growing creative class | Significant — post-pandemic flexible work explosion |

### Key Market Dynamics

**1. The Cafe-Hopper Culture**
HCMC consumers have a "cafe-hopper" behaviour pattern — they visit multiple venues per week, rarely becoming true regulars at any one place. This is the primary churn risk for cafe operators. TEMPO's antidote is community, not caffeine — the regulars who come for connection will anchor the business.

**2. Experiential Shift**
Post-2021, HCMC's F&B market has pivoted toward experience-first venues. Consumers increasingly pay for *how a place makes them feel*, not just for food and drink quality. This is TEMPO's strongest tailwind.

**3. The Day-Night Gap**
Most HCMC venues are either day-only (cafes) or night-only (bars). Very few execute both well under a single brand. The ones that attempt it often feel schizophrenic — the bar feels like an afterthought of the cafe, or vice versa. TEMPO's architectural approach (the day-to-night rhythm as a brand concept, not just an operating schedule) addresses this directly.

**4. The Art Space Deficit**
HCMC lacks quality gallery spaces that integrate with hospitality. The city's art scene is active and growing — but the infrastructure (professional hanging space, climate control, curation expertise, co-mingled F&B) is underdeveloped relative to demand. The Level 2 gallery fills this gap with precision.

**5. Rising Quality Expectations**
The post-pandemic HCMC consumer knows what specialty coffee tastes like. They've been to Melbourne, Singapore, Bangkok. The "good enough" café is no longer enough for the 25–45 professional segment. TEMPO's positioning at the quality ceiling is correctly timed.

## 9. Revenue Architecture

### Revenue Streams — Priority & Margin Analysis

| Stream | Est. Gross Margin | Volume Potential | Defensibility | Priority |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Evening cocktail/wine bar | 65–75% | Medium-High | High | 1 |
| Events & functions (L2) | 70–80% (venue + F&B) | Medium | Very High | 2 |
| Daytime specialty cafe | 35–50% | High | Medium | 3 |
| Gallery commissions (art sales) | 30–50% of sale | Low-Medium | High | 4 |
| Brand partnerships & activations | 80%+ | Low-Medium | Medium | 5 |
| Workshops & ticketed events | 70%+ | Low initially | Medium | 6 |
| Merchandise | 60–70% | Low initially | Medium | 7 |

### Revenue Concentration Risk

At launch, the cafe will likely generate the most transactions but not the most profit. The cocktail bar and events layer are the margin drivers. A conservative year-one model should plan for:

- Cafe: ~50% of revenue transactions, ~30% of gross profit
- Bar: ~35% of revenue transactions, ~45% of gross profit
- Events/Gallery: ~15% of revenue transactions, ~25% of gross profit

As the events pipeline matures (typically 3–6 months after opening), this distribution should shift toward events being a larger share of profit.

### Unit Economics Watchpoints

**Rent:** Two-floor commercial space in HCMC Districts 1–3 will be a significant fixed cost. The combined revenue of all three layers must justify the footprint. Negotiate a rent holiday period for fit-out if possible.

**Staffing structure:** The day-night model requires either:
- Option A: Split teams (day baristas, night bar team) — higher headcount, clearer skills, lower culture cohesion risk
- Option B: Cross-trained staff who transition roles — lower headcount, higher training investment, stronger team culture when done well

Recommendation: Start with split teams for quality control, build cross-training capability over 6–12 months.

**Coffee bean sourcing:** For Melbourne-standard specialty, TEMPO must establish supplier relationships with either:
- Vietnamese specialty producers (recommended — culturally aligned, story-rich)
- Imported roasters (easier for quality consistency but more expensive and less culturally resonant)

## 11. Strategic Opportunities

### Opportunity 1 — The "Founding Community" Programme

**What:** Identify 100 people from the founders' networks — creatives, professionals, food/culture writers, gallery operators — for a structured soft launch. These are not beta testers; they are the founding community.

**Why:** These 100 people will become the brand's most credible advocates. Their word-of-mouth carries more weight than any paid promotion. They also provide real-world feedback during the soft launch window.

**How:** Personal invitations. No public announcement. Experience the full day-to-night model across 2–3 visits. Ask for honest feedback.

### Opportunity 2 — Monthly Exhibition Programme

**What:** A committed monthly or bi-monthly gallery exhibition cycle on Level 2, with opening night events integrated with the bar.

**Why:** Exhibitions create recurring reasons to return (new show = new reason to visit). They create content that tells the brand story. They build the cultural community. They generate press coverage. They create gallery commission revenue.

**How:** Partner with 2–3 curators from the HCMC art community for the first year's programming. Do not curate alone — lean on expertise. Opening night events should be ticketed or by invitation to create selectivity.

### Opportunity 3 — The Events Sales Programme

**What:** A dedicated events capability document, pricing structure, and outreach programme targeting HCMC's brand community.

**Why:** The Level 2 gallery is TEMPO's most defensible and highest-margin asset. It will not book itself.

**Target clients:** Wine importers, specialty food brands, fashion houses, design studios, technology companies, financial services creative teams. All need premium, non-corporate activation venues.

**How:** A simple, beautiful one-page events guide (print and digital). Direct outreach to marketing managers at target companies. A clear inquiry form on the website.

### Opportunity 4 — A "Tempo Circle" Community Model

**What:** An informal but structured community for TEMPO's most engaged regulars — not a points programme, but an access model.

**Why:** The regulars who feel like insiders are the ones who bring their friends, post about it naturally, and defend the brand when the inevitable first negative review appears.

**What it offers:** Early access to events, reserved tables on busy nights, preview invitations to gallery openings, access to member-only workshops.

**How to build it:** Invite the first 50–100 manually, from observation (who comes twice in the first month). Never announce it publicly.

### Opportunity 5 — Strategic Press & Storytelling

**What:** A proactive press outreach strategy for local and international media.

**Why:** TEMPO's founding story (Aussie-Vietnamese, Melbourne + Saigon, three-in-one model) is genuinely compelling editorial content. Good press is the highest-ROI marketing investment for a premium independent venue.

**Timeline:** Begin press outreach 4–6 weeks before opening. Offer exclusives to 1–2 local publications. Time international press for month 2–3 when operations are stable.

## 12. Risks & Mitigation

### Critical Risks

#### Risk 1 — The Physical Experience Doesn't Match the Brand
**Probability:** Medium | **Impact:** Very High

The most dangerous risk. If the physical space — furniture, lighting, music, staff warmth, scent, acoustics — doesn't deliver what the brand deck promises, no amount of excellent social media content will save it.

**Mitigation:**
- Hire a hospitality consultant for the first 4 weeks of soft launch to observe and audit the physical experience
- Staff training is as important as fit-out investment
- Music programming must be curated before opening — not a playlist, a programme
- Do not rush opening. If the space isn't ready, delay.

#### Risk 2 — Operational Undercapacity
**Probability:** Medium | **Impact:** High

Running three distinct offerings simultaneously is demanding. If the events layer has no dedicated owner, it will be deprioritised under day-to-day operational pressure and not achieve its revenue potential.

**Mitigation:**
- Assign explicit ownership: one person owns cafe operations, one owns bar, one owns events/gallery
- Events pipeline should be built 3 months before opening
- Do not hire staff; hire people with the right culture first, skills second

#### Risk 3 — Discovery Gap at Launch
**Probability:** Medium | **Impact:** High

Anti-trend, anti-hype positioning is authentic and correct — but it creates a real risk of speaking to an empty room for too long.

**Mitigation:**
- The founding community programme (100 personal invitations) generates the initial critical mass
- Press outreach ensures there is a narrative available for curious people to find
- Google Business profile optimisation is non-negotiable — this is how the target audience discovers new venues
- Instagram content bank of 30+ pieces ready for the first month

#### Risk 4 — Staff Retention
**Probability:** Medium | **Impact:** Medium-High

HCMC's skilled barista and bartender talent pool is competitive and mobile. TEMPO's warmth must extend to staff culture as much as customer experience.

**Mitigation:**
- Pay above market for key roles (head barista, head bartender)
- Create genuine career development narrative — not just "work at a cool café"
- Staff should feel like the brand as much as customers do

## 13. Strategic Recommendations

### Pre-Launch (Now to Opening Day)

**1. Resolve font licensing.** Monument Grotesk and Editorial New need desktop, web, and print licenses secured before any design agency, printer, or web developer touches the brand. This is a legal and brand quality issue.

**2. Build the events sales capability.** Create a one-page events guide (PDF + web). Reach out to 20 target companies with marketing managers by name. Aim for at least 2–3 events confirmed before opening day.

**3. Curate the first three exhibitions.** The Level 2 gallery's opening programming should be set before the doors open downstairs. Consult with respected HCMC art community voices. These three shows set the cultural tone for the next 12 months.

**4. Build the Google Business Profile completely.** Photos (from fit-out/soft launch), attributes (specialty coffee, cocktail bar, art gallery, private events), bilingual description, correct hours for day/night modes. Do this before the press release goes out.

**5. Develop the bilingual content plan.** Every public-facing touchpoint — social, menu, signage, website — needs a Vietnamese/English strategy confirmed before launch.

**6. Identify and personally invite the Founding 100.** Not a mailing list. Personal invitations. Spread across 2–3 weeks of soft launch. Collect honest feedback.

### Launch Phase (Month 1–3)

**7. Staff experience first, menu second.** The best menu in HCMC served by cold staff will underperform. The opposite can be recovered. Invest training time in warmth, not just skill.

**8. Establish the monthly event cadence.** One gallery event or workshop minimum per month. Announce them 3–4 weeks in advance across all channels. This is TEMPO's content calendar backbone.

**9. Press outreach with the founder story.** Pitch The Saigoneer and Oi Vietnam exclusively (offer first-to-publish). The story is: Aussie-Vietnamese founders bring Melbourne cafe culture home, with a gallery on top. This writes itself.

**10. Activate the Night Tempo programme.** The bar needs its own identity within TEMPO — a curated weekly schedule of music, guest bartenders, or themed evenings. Even if subtle, the intentionality of programming will be felt.

### Growth Phase (Month 4–12)

**11. Launch Tempo Circle (informal members).** Identify your first 50 regulars by observation. Invite them personally to a private event. This is the founding cohort of your community.

**12. Build the brand partnership pipeline.** With 3–6 months of operating history and a growing community, TEMPO will be attractive to brand partners. Create a partnership brief that clearly articulates what collaboration with TEMPO means (and doesn't mean).

**13. Track three P&Ls separately.** Cafe, bar, and events/gallery as distinct cost and revenue centres from month one. This data will show which layer to invest in and which needs strategic attention.

**14. Develop the Vietnamese art partnership programme.** Formalise relationships with RMIT Vietnam, Mỹ Thuật Ho Chi Minh City art community, and 3–4 respected independent galleries for ongoing exhibition programming.
