## 1. Executive Summary

TEMPO House is a multi-format cultural venue at 218c Pasteur, District 3, Ho Chi Minh City — specialty café by day, cocktail bar by night, art gallery on Level 1, private event space on demand. The building is a repurposed Vietnamese shophouse on one of District 3's most characterful colonial streetscapes. The positioning is deliberate: a considered, unhurried counter to Saigon's pace.

This strategy has one job — fill the funnel across all four revenue streams:

1. **Table bookings** — café and bar
2. **Event enquiries** — private hire, corporate, social
3. **Gallery submissions** — artist applications, exhibition interest
4. **Newsletter subscribers** — The TEMPO Letter, audience owned

The approach is editorial-led SEO. TEMPO House is not the kind of place that should rank by volume or spam Google with review-bait. It should rank because the content is genuinely worth reading — and because the venue genuinely earns its authority as a cultural address in District 3.

The content strategy builds around five pillars (Coffee, Bar, Gallery, Event, City) with organic search as the long-term acquisition channel, social media as brand awareness and community, and the newsletter as the retention layer.

## 2. Market & Audience Analysis

### HCMC Specialty Coffee Market

The HCMC specialty coffee market matured sharply between 2019 and 2025. In 2019, "third wave" coffee was still largely the domain of expats and early adopters. By 2025, a younger urban Vietnamese demographic — educated, internationally-traveled, Instagram-native — had shifted the centre of gravity. The market grew from a handful of specialist operators to a saturated field of aesthete cafés, particularly in Districts 1, 3, and Thảo Điền.

Key players that defined the field:

- **L'usine** (District 1 & Lê Thánh Tôn) — pioneer of the lifestyle café concept in HCMC; strong on retail, brunch, art-adjacent positioning. Slightly older demographic now.
- **The Nest by AIA** → now broader lifestyle; early proof of concept for rooftop café culture
- **Bosgaurus Coffee Roasters** — the most serious specialty coffee operation in the city; pure product focus, minimal lifestyle layer
- **Shin Coffee** — premium Vietnamese brand; strong visual identity, suburban and city locations, heavy on social
- **Công Cà Phê** — mass-market nostalgia brand; different category entirely

What distinguishes Melbourne-style specialty coffee from the HCMC mainstream:

- Pour-over and filter as default, not afterthought
- Single-origin transparency (farm, process, tasting notes)
- Barista as knowledge holder, not just order-taker
- Flat white as cultural signifier
- Café as workspace-adjacent, not just pit stop
- Restraint in design — material quality over maximalism

**Why District 3 is a premium café address:** District 3 carries the colonial-era architectural stock that makes for the most photographable café interiors and exteriors in the city. Pasteur, Võ Văn Tần, and Lý Tự Trọng are lined with French-colonial buildings that photograph as "authentic Saigon" while feeling residential and unhurried compared to District 1's commercial intensity. It's where the city's creative class lives and works. It has the energy that brands want to be adjacent to without the noise.

#### Psychographic Profile

The TEMPO House audience is **internationally-traveled, coffee-educated, art-literate, socially conscious, and value-driven**. They are not price-sensitive in the way that mass-market consumers are — they will pay for quality without complaint and will not return to a place that cuts corners. They have opinions about roasters. They know the difference between a Negroni made with cheap vermouth and one that isn't.

They distrust obvious marketing. They trust curation. They follow accounts that teach them something or show them something worth seeing. They share things that make them look like they have good taste — not things that look like ads.

The single most important thing for TEMPO House's content strategy: **earn the share**. Every piece of content — whether it's an Instagram caption, a website article, or a newsletter — should feel like something a person who has good taste would actually want to read or share.

The category TEMPO House competes in — café + art + events as a unified venue concept — is genuinely underserved in HCMC. Most venues do one or two of these well but not three.

TEMPO House is the first to occupy this intersection. That's the content and SEO opportunity: own the compound keywords before anyone else does.

HCMC search behaviour is split across English (expats, international tourists, English-speaking Vietnamese) and Vietnamese. English keywords have significantly lower volume than they do in anglophone markets, which means competition is also lower — domain authority matters less, and well-structured content pages can rank faster. ## 4. Content Pillars

### Pillar 1 — The Coffee

**Core idea:** TEMPO House serves specialty coffee with the same rigour a cocktail bar applies to its spirits. Every cup is a product of decisions — sourcing, processing, brewing. Make those decisions visible.

**Tone:** Educated but not gatekeeping. Confident without being evangelical. The café equivalent of a sommelier who answers the question without making you feel stupid for asking.

**Audience:** Urban Vietnamese professionals, expats, coffee-curious newcomers, digital nomads. The person who just had their first properly dialled flat white and wants to understand why it tasted different.

**Format types:**
- Origin stories (producer profiles, farm visits, processing methods)
- Barista Q&A or interview format
- Brew guides (filter at home, aeropress, the mechanics of extraction)
- Seasonal menu releases
- Coffee + food pairing notes

**Example topics:**
- "What 'washed' actually means — and why it matters in your cup"
- "Why we chose this Ethiopian natural for winter"
- "The flat white question: why Melbourne's most argued-about drink is actually about ratio, not milk"
- "Our new filter menu: three origins, three stories"

---

### Pillar 2 — The Bar

**Core idea:** The bar is the café after dark — same philosophy, different medium. Cocktails are seasonal, considered, and rooted in quality spirits. The atmosphere shifts; the intention doesn't.

**Tone:** Relaxed authority. Not trying to be a craft cocktail tutorial channel — more like a knowledgeable friend recommending something you'll like and explaining why briefly.

**Audience:** Saigon nightlife audience that's bored of the same gin menus, expats who drink well at home, content creators looking for evening aesthetic content.

**Format types:**
- Seasonal menu reveals
- Cocktail spotlights (the spirit, the idea, the method)
- Behind-the-bar photography series
- Evening programming announcements
- Pairing guides (cocktail + small plates)

**Example topics:**
- "The aperitivo hour: why we added a Spritz menu and what we put in it"
- "New season menu: six cocktails, one idea"
- "Why we're not doing Happy Hour — and what we do instead"
- "The house Negroni: three ingredients, one choice that changes everything"

---

### Pillar 3 — The Gallery

**Core idea:** Level 1 is a curated programme, not a rotating wall of whatever-fits. The gallery is a genuine cultural commitment — to the artists it shows, to the audience it builds, and to the conversation it starts.

**Tone:** Art-adjacent but accessible. TEMPO House is not a white-cube gallery with a wall text that needs translating. It's a place where someone who doesn't "get" art yet can encounter it and leave with something.

**Audience:** Art enthusiasts, creative professionals, KOLs, gallery-goers, art collectors, emerging artists looking for exhibition space.

**Format types:**
- Artist profiles (pre-exhibition builds, "meet the artist")
- Exhibition walkthroughs (written + visual)
- Artist-in-conversation content (interview, studio visit)
- Opening night coverage
- Call for submissions

**Example topics:**
- "Meet the artist: [Name] on memory, material, and making work in Saigon"
- "Opening night: [Exhibition Name] — what to know before you come"
- "The gallery is open for submissions — what we're looking for in 2026"
- "Five works to see this month"

---

### Pillar 4 — The Event

**Core idea:** Every event at TEMPO House is an opportunity to show the venue working at its best. Behind-the-scenes builds, post-event photography, and real event stories are more convincing than any brochure.

**Tone:** Confident and editorial. Show, don't just tell. Let the photography carry the emotional weight; let the copy explain the details that photos can't.

**Audience:** Event planners, brand managers, couples, marketing teams, anyone who has looked at TEMPO House and wondered "could this work for my event?"

**Format types:**
- Post-event case studies ("how we hosted [Brand X's] product launch")
- Before + after photography features
- Venue setup guides ("how the gallery transforms for a seated dinner")
- Client testimonials (integrated editorial-style, not review widget)
- Frequently asked questions (capacity, catering, AV, access)

**Example topics:**
- "How a Saigon fashion brand launched their new collection at TEMPO House"
- "The gallery, the terrace, and 60 guests: how [Wedding] worked"
- "What 150 people standing actually looks like in the full venue"
- "Seven things to think about when booking a product launch venue in HCMC"

---

### Pillar 5 — The City

**Core idea:** TEMPO House is inseparable from District 3. The building, the street, the neighbourhood — these are not backdrop. They're context. Content about the city earns search traffic, builds local authority, and places TEMPO House within a broader cultural landscape.

**Tone:** Insider guide. The kind of content that a smart friend who lives in D3 would write — opinionated, specific, honest about what to skip.

**Audience:** Tourists, expats new to HCMC, Vietnamese readers looking for a local perspective, lifestyle media.

**Format types:**
- Neighbourhood guides (Pasteur Street, D3 evening circuit)
- "What we're listening to / reading / drinking this week" roundups
- HCMC arts calendar (monthly)
- Saigon lifestyle editorial (seasonal, cultural)
- Community features (other D3 businesses, artists, makers)

**Example topics:**
- "District 3 after dark: a considered guide to a Tuesday evening"
- "The Pasteur block: what's here, what's worth stopping for"
- "Saigon's art calendar: what's on in June"
- "Why District 3 remains the most interesting part of Ho Chi Minh City"

## 6. Technical SEO Checklist

### Schema Markup Priorities

Implement these in order:

1. **LocalBusiness schema** — On homepage and /contact. Include: `name`, `address`, `telephone`, `email`, `url`, `openingHoursSpecification` (separate for café and bar hours), `geo`, `priceRange`, `servesCuisine` (Specialty Coffee), `hasMap`.

2. **ArtGallery schema** — On /gallery and /events/art-exhibitions. Extends `LocalBusiness`. Include: `event` array linking to current exhibitions.

3. **Event schema** — On each individual event listing within /whats-on. Include: `name`, `startDate`, `endDate`, `location` (with full address), `description`, `image`, `organizer`, `eventStatus`, `eventAttendanceMode`.

4. **FoodEstablishment schema** — On /cafe for the café context and /bar for the bar. Include menu URL when available.

5. **BreadcrumbList** — On all sub-pages (/events/product-launch etc). Critical for Tier 3 pages.

### Image Optimisation

**File naming convention:**
- Use descriptive, keyword-rich file names, lowercase, hyphenated
- Format: `[subject]-[location/context]-tempohouse.[ext]`
- Examples:
  - `specialty-coffee-district-3-tempohouse.jpg`
  - `gallery-level-1-event-setup-tempohouse.jpg`
  - `cocktail-bar-evening-pasteur-tempohouse.jpg`
  - NOT: `IMG_4821.jpg`, `DSC0042.jpg`, `photo1.png`

**Alt text strategy:**
- Every image must have alt text. No exceptions.
- Alt text should describe what is literally in the image, with natural keyword inclusion where it fits
- Good: `"Single-origin pour-over coffee being brewed at TEMPO House specialty café, District 3"`
- Bad: `"coffee"`, `"photo of the bar"`, `"interior"` (empty or keyword-stuffed)
- Gallery images: name the artwork and artist — `"Abstract mixed media piece by [Artist Name] on display at TEMPO Gallery, Level 1"`

**Format guidance:**
- Hero and lifestyle photography: AVIF or WebP, compressed to <150KB
- Event thumbnails: WebP, <80KB
- Logo/UI elements: SVG where possible
- Never serve full-resolution camera exports (3–5MB JPGs will tank Core Web Vitals)

### WordPress Site Speed

TEMPO House is running on WordPress. Core Web Vitals are a ranking factor. Minimum viable technical setup:

- **Caching plugin:** WP Rocket or LiteSpeed Cache — enable page caching, browser caching, GZIP compression
- **Image delivery:** Offload to CDN (Cloudflare + R2, or Bunny CDN which has APAC edge nodes better suited to Vietnamese users). Use a lazy loading plugin for below-fold images.
- **Font loading:** Self-host any custom fonts — Google Fonts served from Google's CDN will add DNS lookup latency. Use `font-display: swap`.
- **Unused CSS/JS:** Minify and defer non-critical JavaScript. Remove any plugin-registered scripts on pages that don't use them.
- **LCP target:** The hero image is likely the Largest Contentful Paint element. Pre-load it with `<link rel="preload">` and serve it at the correct display size, not full-res.
- **Core Web Vitals baseline:** Run PageSpeed Insights on launch. Target: LCP <2.5s, CLS <0.1, INP <200ms on mobile.

## 7. Social Media & KOL Strategy

### Instagram

Instagram is TEMPO House's primary visual channel and the one most likely to drive direct foot traffic and event enquiries. It should feel like the venue's editorial identity in motion — not a menu board, not a promotional feed.

**Content cadence:** 4–5 posts per week + 10–14 Stories per week + 2–3 Reels per week

**Visual format — photography direction:**
- Natural light for daytime café content — morning light on the bar counter, coffee on white/marble surface, exterior architecture at golden hour
- Atmospheric, moody for bar and evening content — low key lighting, bokeh, texture
- Gallery content: clean, well-lit, artwork-forward — let the work speak, support with artist context in caption
- People in the frame matter: candid over posed, guests reading/working/talking rather than holding coffee and looking at camera
- No flat lays. No overhead food shots. Not the visual language of the brand.

**Grid approach:** No rigid colour-lock needed — the architecture and design language will do that naturally. Aim for rhythm: coffee → people → space → detail. Avoid three identical bar shots in a row.

**Hashtag strategy:**

Primary (high volume, competitive — use 2–3 per post):
`#hcmc #saigon #hochiminh #vietnam`

Secondary (medium volume, relevant):
`#specialtycoffee #saigoncafe #hcmccafe #district3 #quận3 #cafehochiminh`

Niche (lower volume, higher intent — use 4–6 per post):
`#melbournecoffee #specialtycafesaigon #cocktailbarsaigon #saigonbar #artgalleryhcmc #saigoneventspace #artexhibitionsaigon`

Brand:
`#tempohouse #tempohousgn #wherethecitieslowsdown`

**Caption philosophy:** Write one thing worth reading. Not a list of menu items. Not "swipe left for more!" Not "DM us to book." Tell the story behind the image — the origin of the coffee, the artist in the show, the idea behind the cocktail. Call to action in the last line only, and only when there's a clear action worth taking.

### TikTok

TikTok's strength for TEMPO House is the time-of-day narrative. The venue genuinely transforms across the day — this is a content series, not a one-off.

**Series concept: "One address, three worlds"**

Structure each video around the transformation:
- **Morning (7am):** Coffee prep, opening ritual, first light through windows, barista setting up
- **Afternoon (1pm–4pm):** Gallery hours, natural light, work-from-café atmosphere, art detail shots

Each video in the series should be 30–60 seconds, minimal voiceover, strong sound design. The concept rewards return viewers because they understand the format and look forward to the next instalment.

**Other TikTok formats:**
- Cocktail makes — 15–30 second process videos, no tutorial narration needed
- Coffee origin explainers — short education content ("this coffee is from Ethiopia, this is what that means in the cup")

## 8. Newsletter Strategy — The TEMPO Letter

### Name

**The TEMPO Letter** — editorial, unhurried, named for the venue. Not "TEMPO News" or "Our Newsletter." The name should feel like it belongs in an inbox.

### Cadence

**Monthly, on a fixed send date.** Not event-driven — event-driven newsletters become irregular and train subscribers to expect silence. The 1st or 15th of each month works; the 1st creates a "start of month" reading ritual.

One exception: gallery opening nights get a standalone send, 5–7 days prior, specifically for that audience segment.

### Content Format

Each issue of The TEMPO Letter should read like a short magazine — not a list of promotions. Structure:

1. **Opening note (150–200 words):** One observation about this time of year, the city, the season, the art scene. Voice: the venue itself, not a staff member. Think of it as an editorial from the address.

2. **What's On:** Upcoming exhibitions, events, bar specials, and new menu items. Short, list-style, linked.

3. **The Gallery:** Brief on the current or upcoming exhibition — the artist, the idea, the dates. One image.

4. **From the Bar:** Seasonal menu update, one featured cocktail, any guest bartender or takeover events.

5. **From the Kitchen/Café:** New origin on the filter menu, or a coffee/food pairing note.

6. **District 3:** One recommendation from the neighbourhood — a neighbouring business, an event, a cultural happening. Builds goodwill and community.

### List Growth Strategy

- **On-site capture (primary):** A physical card at the café counter and bar: "The TEMPO Letter — monthly notes from the address. Scan to subscribe." QR code links to a minimal landing page or embedded form.
- **Digital capture:** Homepage opt-in (non-intrusive — footer or a small, well-designed overlay on second visit, not on load)
- **Instagram Stories:** Monthly "subscribe" CTA in Stories, with link sticker. Time it around newsletter send day.
- **Event guests:** At all events, include a "stay in the loop" QR code on the event brief/programme
- **Gallery openings:** Offer artists and attendees a simple sign-up card at the opening — they're already invested in the cultural programming

### Segmentation

Segment the list into three groups (implement from day one — retrofitting later is painful):

| Segment | Description | Tailored content |
|---|---|---|
| **Café & Bar regulars** | General audience, walk-in visitors, social media followers | Full letter — all sections |
| **Event & venue inquirers** | Anyone who has submitted an event enquiry or attended a hosted event | Prioritise event news, venue updates, new gallery-as-event-space content |
| **Gallery & art audience** | Subscribers who came through gallery opening or exhibition content | Prioritise gallery section, artist features, exhibition calendar, art-specific events |

Do not over-engineer segmentation early. Three buckets is enough for the first 12 months.
